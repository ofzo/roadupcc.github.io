Plural 复数

To make most nouns plural, add an s at the end.

For words that end in x, sh, s, ss and ch, add es at the end.

Use a few for three or four items.

Use be + from to talk about where someone is from. Use live + in to talk about where someone lives. Remember to add s to the end of live for he, she and it.

Nouns are used to refer to countries; adjectives, to describe nationality or language. To change the noun to an adjective, a suffix is added, the most common being -ian, -ese and -ish.

Use what’s + noun + like questions to get a description or opinion of a city or country.

You can also use how questions to ask someone’s opinion about a city or country.

Use the articles a and an before a non-specific, singular noun or the adjective that comes before a singular noun.

Some numbers in English sound very similar. These are the -ty and -teen numbers, like eighty (80) and eighteen (18). Notice how the first syllable is stressed in the -ty numbers, whereas the syllables are evenly stressed in the -teen numbers.

Use how many to ask about quantity. In how many questions, the noun is always plural.

Notice that some nouns, like the word people, are irregular plurals and don’t end in s.

Use the present continuous for actions that are happening now. Form the present continuous with the verb be and a verb + ing.

Use the phrase made of to talk about a fabric. Clothing labels often have a percentage sign next to a fabric name when fabrics are blended.

Adverbs

Always and never are adverbs of frequency. They tell us how often something happens. These adverbs come after the verb be in a sentence but in front of all other verbs.

Some adverbs, such as really and very, make an adjective or verb stronger. Use really in front of an adjective or a verb. Use very only in front of an adjective.

Another way to make an adjective stronger is to add the adverb so before it.

Use want + noun to say that somebody wants something. Remember to add s to want after he, she or it.
